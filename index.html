😀World Happiness (2015-2022)😀

By Sami Hussein Hamid Saeed

An analysis of the Happiness levels around the world and the various factors that affect it, gathered from several different data sources and collected from citizens from each country. The Happiness Index measures life satisfaction, the feeling of happiness, and other happiness domains such as: health, finanical status, government trust, freedom, generosity, and family.

Happiness World Map

Using the following map you can view the change in happiness in the entire worldthroughout the years 2015 to 2022, using the filters you can also limit the data to show the top 10 or 100 happiest countries for any year. From the map bellow we can analyse that on average, the happiest countries in the world tend to be in Europe and Oceania, this could be attributed to the significantly advanced quality of life, government trust, economical stability, and high health standards in these continents. Furthermore, North America and South America both have a consistently good happiness score throughout the years 2015-2022, although over the years, the happiness levels in South America have somewhat detoriorated. Finally, the happiness levels in Asia and especially Africa are one of lowest levels in the entire world, regardless of the year.

Happiest Countries Of The World

From the country rankings bellow we can observe that the happiest countries in the world are mostly from Oceania and Europe (mainly Scandanavia). Another interesting observation is that for the years 2015 to 2021, no countries from Asia, Africa, or South America made it into the leaderboard. But in 2022, Israel has made it into the leaderboard, representing Asia. Finally, until the year 2019 Canada was the only country from North America to make it into the top 10 happiest countries in the world.

Happiest Countries In Each Continent

As shown in the lollipop chart to the right, we can observe the change in happiness levels for each specific continent throughout the years 2015 to 2022.

Firstly, in North America, Canada is consistently the most happiest country in the continent and although the United States is the most famous country in North America it pales in comparision to Canada and Costa Rica (2015-2021). Even though Europe has one of the highest happiness averages, it is actually mainly because of the presence of Scandanavian countries. In Asia, Israel is always the happiest country in the continent, the rest of the countries are mostly middle eastern countries.

Moreover, the data available for Oceania is only for Australia and New Zealand, therefore Oceania has a consistently high happiness level. Contrary to popular belief South America actually has a surprisingly high happiness average. Finally, Africa has the lowest levels of happiness amongst all of the continents.

Progress Made By Each Continent

The slope chart to the left showcases the overall progress in happiness made by each continent from the year 2015 to the year 2022 based on the happiness score index. Overall, we can observe a positive change in the happiness levels for each continent, with the exception of South America and Oceania, despite the overall positive trends worldwide

Oceania's happiness score has went down from 7.29 in the year 2015 to 7.18 in the year 2022. In addition, South America's happiness score has also decreased from 6.35 in the year 2015 to a shocking 5.79 in the year 2022.

North America and Asia have shown slight improvements in their respective happiness. However Europe and Africa have both thrived and are showing more promising signs of improvement in their respective happiness levels in 2022

Key Happiness Factors

There are a lot of factors that affect the happiness levels of a country, such as: economy, freedom, health, generosity, family, and trust in government. In order to further analyse the relationships between these factors and happiness. In general Europe, Oceania , and South America tend to have a high happiness score, whereas North America, Asia , and Africa have lower levels of happiness in comparision.

Generosity

The extent to which Generosity contributed to the calculation of the Happiness Score. Generosity will increase the psychological well being of everyone involved while also having an impact on material wellbeing, which are two of the domains of Gross National Happiness. If we want there to be change in our countries happiness, it starts by making other people happy.

Government

The extent to which Perception of Corruption contributes to Happiness Score. Happier countries tend to have a high level of trust in their respective governments, this is mainly because these governments are providing for their citizens and are hence proving to their people that they are worthy of their trust. The people of a country must be able to rely on and trust their leaders and governments in order to lead a stress free way of life.

Freedom

The extent to which Perception of Freedom in a country contributes to Happiness Score.Freedom is important for our happiness because it allows us to make choices that make us happy and gives us the opportunity to make mistakes and learn from them. So our happiness depends on how we feel about the choices we've made and continue to make in life.

Health

The extent to which health and life expectancy contributes to Happiness Score.The countries that score high on the quality and accessibility of their often freely available healthcare generally all score high on the happiness index. Most of the happiest countries in the world providy easily available healthcare and ackowledge healthcare as a basic human right, rather than a commerical industry.

Family

The extent to which family contributes to Happiness Score. Strong, happy families create a sense of well being within the family and in each family member. Wellbeing includes a person's physical, mental, emotional and social health. Strong, happy families encourage and support each other to be optimistic, hopeful and to strive together to achieve individually and as a family.

Economy

The extent to which economy contributes to Happiness Score. The economy of a country plays a significantly important role in the happiness of the country, this is mainly due to the ability of wealthy countries to facilitate their citizens and satisfy their needs. People in wealthier countries with high-quality institutions tend to be happier than people in countries with less wealth and poorer institutions.

Happiness In Malaysia

Comparing Malaysia's key happiness factors and their respective magnitudes with respect to Asia's average score for each factor shows That in each and every category other than government trust, Malaysia scores higher than the average displayed by the Asian countries in general.
